Medicines are taken to help keep your symptoms under control or to prevent or treat a heart condition.

What do the medicines do?

Most medicines used to treat heart conditions change how the heart or the blood circulation.

People are all different and it can be difficult to know exactly which medicine may suit you. You should take your medication as prescribed and if you feel any adverse side-effects report them to your doctor.

Understanding your medication

There are some basic questions to know before you start to take any new medicine. The more informed you are about your medication the better.

ASK:

  • What is the medication for?
  • What are the benefits and risks?
  • How should you take the medicine for best results?
  • What are the possible side effects and what to do if you notice any?
  • Does this new medication approved to take with other kinds of medicine at the same time, including any over-the-counter medicines or supplements.

How are heart medicines taken?

  • Tablets or capsules - These need to be swallowed with or possibly dissolved in water. Sometimes you have to keep the tablet under your tongue until it dissolves.
  • Aerosol spray - You spray the medicine under your tongue
  • Self-adhesive patch - A patch containing your medicine is placed on your skin and the medicine is absorbed

When will I need to take my medication?

Most medications need to be taken regularly; however, some will need to be taken when you experience a symptom such as angina. You should always follow your doctor's instructions
